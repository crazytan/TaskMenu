# TaskMenu Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** March 10, 2026

TaskMenu is a free, open-source macOS menu bar app for managing Google Tasks. Your privacy matters — here's how TaskMenu handles your data.

## What TaskMenu accesses

TaskMenu connects to the [Google Tasks API](https://developers.google.com/tasks) to read and write your tasks and task lists. This is the only external service the app communicates with.

## Where your data is stored

All data stays on your device:

- **OAuth tokens** are stored in the macOS Keychain, protected by your system's security.
- **Task data** is fetched from Google on demand and displayed in the app. It is not cached or stored separately on disk.

## What TaskMenu does NOT do

- **No analytics or telemetry.** The app does not track how you use it.
- **No data collection.** TaskMenu does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information beyond what is needed to authenticate with Google.
- **No third-party services.** The app only communicates with Google APIs directly. No data is sent to any other server or service.
- **No ads.** There are no advertisements in TaskMenu.

## Google OAuth

TaskMenu uses Google's OAuth 2.0 to sign you in. During sign-in, Google may show you what data TaskMenu is requesting access to. You can revoke TaskMenu's access at any time from your [Google Account permissions page](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).
